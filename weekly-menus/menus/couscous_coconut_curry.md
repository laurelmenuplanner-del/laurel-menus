# Couscous Coconut Curry (All; GF) / Chicken Breast (Ca) / Baked Tofu (Veg, Vn) / Roasted Broccoli (All) / Soy Garlic Salad (All)

## Couscous Coconut Curry (All) Yield: 40

### Ingredients:

- ¼ cup olive oil
- 3 yellow onions
- 15 cloves garlic, minced
- 1.5 tablespoons minced ginger
- 3 carrots, finely diced
- 112oz canned chickpeas (7x16oz cans)
- 2x4 oz Thai Kitchen Gluten Free Green Curry Paste
- 136 oz canned coconut milk (10 13.66oz cans)
- 7 cups vegetable stock (instructions depend on vegetable base used)
- 1.75 cups coconut sugar
- 2 tablespoon lime zest (2-4 limes)
- Juice of 6 limes
- 6 cups dry Israeli couscous
- 1 tablespoon salt, to taste
- 1 bunches cilantro, chopped

### Instructions:

1. Finely chop onions and dice carrots
2. Peel skin off of the ginger and mince. Mince garlic as well.
3. Drain and rinse chickpeas. Pat dry with paper towels.
4. Heat the tilt skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add coconut oil, garlic, ginger, carrot and onion.
5. Cook, stirring frequently, for 5-10 minutes or until onion is translucent. Add curry paste and stir to coat. Cook for 5 minutes
6. Add chickpeas and stir to coat once more. Cook for 5 minutes.
7. Add coconut milk, vegetable broth, coconut sugar (or maple syrup), lime zest, lime juice, and stir to combine.
8. Bring mixture to a heavy simmer over medium-high heat.
   - RESERVE A SMALL AMOUNT OF THIS SAUCE FOR THE GF OPTION.
9. Once simmering, add in couscous, stir and reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20 minutes.
10. Taste and adjust seasonings as needed, adding fresh lime juice for tang, maple syrup or coconut sugar for added sweetness, and salt for depth of flavor.
11. Then turn off heat and let rest for at least 10 minutes before serving. This will allow the flavor to permeate the chickpeas and couscous even more.
12. Transfer one-tenth (10%) into a smaller hotel pan. This is for the veggies/vegans.
13. Mix chicken into the large hotel pan with couscous.
14. Garnish both with fresh cilantro
15. Serve in hot trays!

## Baked Chicken Breast (Ca) Yield: 40

### Ingredients:

- 16 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breast, pounded to even thickness (3 sam's club trays)
- 2 cups of olive oil
- 2 tablespoons of salt
- 2 tablespoon of pepper
- 5 tablespoons of garlic powder
- 5 tablespoons of onion powder
- 5 tablespoons of paprika

### Instructions:

1. If frozen, thaw chicken breasts in meat sink to defrost.
2. Pound chicken breasts with the tenderizer to uniform thickness. If you can't find the tenderizer hammer then cut the huge breasts to be the size of the smallest breast.
3. Preheat the oven to 375 F.
4. Combine spices and olive oil into one medium to large bowl (you may need to add more olive oil).
5. Use cooking oil or olive oil to grease baking sheets.
6. In a large bowl combine the thawed chicken breasts and spice/oil mixture until the chicken is well coated. Let the chicken sit and marinate for 30 minutes.
7. Place chicken breasts on the baking sheet and bake in a preheated oven for 16-20 minutes. Check periodically. Check the internal temperature of the chicken. It has to be 165F (DO NOT SERVE IF IT IS NOT 165F).
8. *MAKE SURE TO CHECK THE CHICKEN TEMP IN THE THICKEST PART OF THE CHICKEN*
9. Let cool until handleable. Cut into bite-size pieces. Mix into the carnivore couscous.

## Baked Tofu (Veg, Vn) Yield: 4

### Ingredients:

- 4 lbs of tofu
- 1 ½ tbsp salt
- 3 tbsp black pepper
- 4 ½ tbsp garlic powder
- Olive oil
- 3 cups cornstarch

### Instructions:

1. Preheat oven to 400
2. Press blocks of tofu by placing paper towels on cutting boards and placing tofu on top. Place another cutting board on top of tofu and set some heavy cans on top to provide further weight.
3. Press for 20 minutes, then cut tofu into small bite-sized pieces.
4. Place tofu in a large bowl mix in 1 cup of olive oil and season with salt, garlic powder, cornstarch, and pepper.
5. Place tofu on a baking tray greased with olive oil and bake at 400F for 20 minutes. Until the tofu becomes crispy. (CHECK PERIODICALLY TO MAKE SURE IT DOES NOT BURN)
6. Serve portions in a small hotel pan

## Roasted Garlic Broccoli (All) Yield: 40

### Ingredients:

- 7lbs broccoli florets
- 1.5 cups olive oil
- 30 cloves garlic
- 0.5-1 tablespoons salt
- 2 tablespoons pepper

### Instructions:

1. Chop broccoli into bite-sized pieces. Mince the garlic (run through food processor twice)
2. Pre-heat oven to 375 F
3. Mix broccoli, olive oil, garlic, and salt and pepper together in large bowl.
4. Grease baking sheets with olive oil and spread broccoli onto baking sheets.
5. Bake for 12-17 minutes or until cooked through and slightly browned.
   - Check periodically to ensure it doesn't burn!

## Soy Garlic Salad (All): Yield: 40

### Ingredients:

- ½ of a sysco spring mix pillow (1.5 lbs)
- 2 cucumbers, sliced
- 5 oz (~1 ½ cups) matchstick carrots = half of an HEB 10oz matchstick carrots bag

#### Dressing:

- ¾ cups Tamari gluten-free soy sauce
- ⅜ cup water
- ¼ cup mirin
- ½ cup + 1 tablespoon brown sugar
- 8 cloves garlic, minced
- 1.5 tbsp sesame oil

### Instructions:

1. Mince the garlic. You can use a grater or the food processor.
2. Slice the cucumbers and shred the carrots
3. In a bowl, combine the dressing ingredients and whisk together until completely combined.
4. Pour mixture into a saucepan and bring the liquid to a gentle boil.
5. Reduce heat to medium and let it simmer for 8 minutes.
6. Remove from heat and cool the dressing in the fridge for 15 minutes.
7. In a large serving bowl, combine the salad, sliced cucumbers, and shredded carrots together.
8. Serve the salad in a cold tray with dressing on the side!!
