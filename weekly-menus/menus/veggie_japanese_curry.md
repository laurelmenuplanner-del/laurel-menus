# Veggie Japanese Curry / White Rice

**NOTE: Read the directions first. For instance, soaking the potatoes (step 4 during prep requires soaking the potatoes first)**

## Japanese Curry Yield: 35

### Ingredients:

- 2 ¼ tsp kosher salt (Diamond Crystal; use half for table salt)
- Freshly ground black pepper
- 8 carrots
- 4 onions
- 12 Yukon gold potatoes
- 2 knobs ginger
- 6 cloves garlic
- 6 apples, cut in half
- Curry base
  - 11.25 Tbsp honey
  - 17tsp (or 6tbsp) of better than bouillon with 17cups of water for the veggie stock.
  - 7 tbsp soy sauce
  - 6 tbsp ketchup
  - The curry roux you make
- Curry Roux:
  - 3 cups + 3 Tbsp unsalted butter
  - 5 cups flour
  - 1 cup + 13 Tbsp curry powder
  - 6 Tbsp + 2 tsp chili powder
  - 4 tsp cayenne pepper
- Curry Roux (Gluten-Free):
  - 2 tbsp of vegan butter
  - 3 tbsp of gluten-free flour
  - 2 tbsp of curry powder
  - 1 tsp of garam masala
  - ⅛ tsp of cayenne pepper

## Directions (curry roux prep):

**NOTE: Making the curry roux for GF and non-GF is the same. Use a separate pot / pan to make the gluten-free curry roux.**

1. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over low to medium-low heat (you can also cut the butter into small pieces first).
2. In another small pan, melt
3. When the butter is completely melted, add the flour. With a blunt-end wooden spatula, stir to combine the butter and flour.
4. Soon, the butter and flour will fuse and swell. Cook for 20-25 minutes over low heat. Stir constantly because the roux burns easily. If the roux starts separating, switch to a whisk and mix vigorously.
5. Add the curry powder, garam masala, and cayenne pepper to the roux.
   - NOTE: The cayenne pepper is to your discretion. It should be enough to make it slightly spicy, but not too much. The volume we are cooking at is too much for me to give you a specific number.
6. Cook and stir for 30 seconds and remove from the heat

## Directions (main curry prep/cooking):

1. Dice the onions
2. Peel the carrots and cut them into rolling wedges. This type of cut creates more surface area, which helps the carrots absorb more flavor and cook faster.
3. Peel the potatoes. Cut each potato into quarters (6 pieces for russet potatoes). Soak them in water for 15 minutes to remove excess starch. Pat dry once they are removed from the water.
4. Grate the ginger. You will need 1 tsp of grated ginger, including the juice. Mince the garlic and set aside (I use a garlic press later when adding to the pot).
5. Cut the apple into quarters, remove the core, and peel the skin.
6. Grate the apple into small bits. You can use a grater, but using just a knife and cutting finely will probably help not turn the apple to mush)
7. Heat the oil in the tilt skillet over medium heat and add the onion.
8. Sauté the onions, stirring occasionally, until they become translucent and tender, about 5 minutes. Don't stir the onions too often because they won't develop a golden color. Cooked onions add amazing flavor, so we do not want to skip this step. If you have an extra 5 minutes, definitely caramelize the onions!
9. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and mix well together. If the onions are getting too brown, reduce the heat to medium low temporarily.
10. Add the stock by adding the better than bouillon with the water.
11. Add the grated apple, honey, soy sauce, and ketchup
12. Add the carrots and potatoes. The liquid should barely cover the vegetables. Do not add extra liquid; the vegetables will release moisture as they cook.
13. Simmer, covered, on medium-low heat for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Simmer uncovered if the ingredients are completely submerged in the broth.
14. Once boiling, use a to skim the scum and foam from the surface of the broth.
15. Continue to cook covered until a wooden skewer (or anything else that is long and skinny) goes through the carrots and potatoes.

### FINISHING THE GLUTEN-FREE CURRY (DO THIS FIRST)

1. Using a clean, GF-only ladle, transfer 2 portions of curry base into a separate GF pot.
2. Turn heat OFF.
3. Place a portion of the GF curry roux into a ladle (attached; below).
4. Add a small amount of hot cooking liquid to the roux and stir until smooth.
5. Stir the loosened roux into the GF pot.
6. Simmer gently, uncovered, for 5–10 minutes, stirring with a GF utensil.
7. Taste and adjust salt if needed.
8. Cover, hold warm, and serve in a separate (small) container.

### FINISHING THE NON-GLUTEN-FREE CURRY

1. Turn off heat on the main pot.
2. Place a portion of the non-GF roux into a ladle.
3. Add hot cooking liquid to dissolve, then stir into the main pot.
4. Repeat until all roux is incorporated.
5. Simmer uncovered on medium-low heat for 5–10 minutes, stirring frequently.
6. Adjust thickness with water if needed.
7. Taste and adjust salt.
8. Serve in its own container. Use the single-serve hotel pan.
