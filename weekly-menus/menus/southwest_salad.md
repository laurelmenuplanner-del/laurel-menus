# Broccoli Pesto Pasta

** We have a gluten allergy, so let's take the first step seriously. Boil the gluten-free pasta first and put it cooked away from everything else. Please be sure to not use the different utensils for the gluten-free option than you do for the regular pasta, try to prevent cross contamination as much as possible. Thanks!

## Broccoli Pesto Pasta Yield: 35

### Ingredients:

- 80 oz of penne pasta
- 1 pack gluten-free pasta
- 3 heads of broccoli, chopped into small florets
- 16 teaspoon minced garlic
- 4 chopped white onion
- 3 cup of parmesan cheese
- 5 cup Spinach chopped
- 1 eggplants
- 2 green zucchini
- Pepper/salt/garlic/red pepper to taste
- Olive oil for taste and cooking

### Handmade Pesto Ingredients

- 4 cups fresh basil leaves (no stems)
- ⅖ cup + 3Tbsp walnuts
- 10 teaspoons of mined garlic
- 1 ¾ cups freshly grated parmesan cheese
- 1 cups extra-virgin olive oil

(if no handmade pesto ingredients, then use premade pesto)

### Directions:

#### Making the Pesto

1. Combine all of the pesto ingredients besides the olive oil in a blender, blending until a smooth paste is formed. This paste should be thicker than normal.
2. Scoop out this thick pesto paste into a large bowl, then add the olive oil to it. After stirring, the pesto should be slightly thinner, but still at sauce thickness.
3. Be aware that this may be more pesto than needed. Use your judgment at the end—when adding the pesto to the pasta—to decide if to use all of it

- At this point, start boiling the water to cook the pasta in. Look at the needed amount of water on the package and scale up according to how many packages we are using.

#### Broccoli:

1. Dice the onions.
   - Note: You can speed this part quickly by cutting the onions into quarters and throwing inside a food processor. When inside the processor, pulse a few times.
2. Add olive oil and spices to the tilt skillet.
   - I would recommend italian seasoning, red pepper flakes, and basil. Don't add too much for this step, as the sausage is already spiced, but keep these spices out for later in the recipe
3. After heating up the oil and spices on high heat, add in the broccoli. Keep the broccoli moving to allow it to gain color on all sides.
4. Add the onions and garlic into the pan to cook with the broccoli. Cook until they have sautéd, probably around 10 minutes.
5. Remove the broccoli mixture and set aside, you will add it back in later.

#### Finishing the Pasta

 The water should be done boiling now. Add the pasta once this happens.

1. Add in the eggplant, zucchini, and spinach to the tilt skillet, allowing the pieces of spice from the broccoli mix to cook in with the vegetables.
2. Cook the vegetables on medium heat for about 10-15 minutes, or until the zucchini becomes very soft.
3. Once everything is cooked down, add the broccoli mixture back in, and reduce heat so that there is only a light simmer in the pan.
4. Once the noodles are done (according to the package directions), add them to the tilt skillet as well. Immediately after, add the appropriate amount of pesto.
5. After stirring the mixture to completely combine all of the ingredients, add parmesan until there is a bit of stickiness to the noodles. Don't add too much, but also don't skimp.
6. Move the finished pasta to a hotel pan and set out on the hot table with a lid on it

## Caesar Salad Yield: 35

### Ingredients:

#### Caesar Salad dressing

- 14 teaspoon minced garlic
- ¼ cup +4 Tbsp dijon mustard
- 1/4 cup Worcestershire sauce
- ¼ cup +3 Tbsp fresh lemon juice
- ¼ cup red wine vinegar
- 4 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 3 Tbsp sea salt, or to taste
- 3 Tbsp black pepper, plus more to serve

#### Cesar Salad

- 3 large romaine lettuce
- 7 cup parmesan cheese, shredded or shaved (on the side)

### Directions

#### For the dressing:

1. In a small bowl, whisk together garlic, dijon, Worcestershire, lemon juice and red wine vinegar.
2. Slowly drizzle in extra virgin olive oil while whisking constantly.
3. Whisk in salt and black pepper, or season to taste.

#### For the salad:

1. Rinse, dry and chop or tear the romaine into bite-sized pieces. Place in a large serving bowl
2. Top with dressing and toss gently until lettuce is evenly coated.
3. Serve in cold table with the parmesan cheese on the side
