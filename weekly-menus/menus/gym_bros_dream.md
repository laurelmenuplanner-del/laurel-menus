## Baked Tofu (Veg, Vn) Yield: 5

### Ingredients:

- 3 lbs of tofu
- 1/2 tbsp salt
- 1 tbsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp garlic powder
- 3/4 cups olive oil
- 1 cups cornstarch

### Directions:

1. Preheat oven to 400
2. Press blocks of tofu by placing paper towels on cutting boards and placing tofu on top. Place another cutting board on top of tofu and set some heavy cans on top to provide further weight.
3. Press for 20 minutes, then cut tofu into small bite-sized pieces.
4. Place tofu in a large bowl mix in 1 cup of olive oil and season with salt, garlic powder, cornstarch, and pepper.
5. Place tofu on baking tray greased with olive oil and bake at 400F for 20 minutes. Until the tofu becomes crispy. (CHECK PERIODICALLY TO MAKE SURE IT DOES NOT BURN)
6. Serve portions in a small hotel pan

## Roasted Vegetables (All) Yield: 70

### Ingredients:

- 14 medium bell peppers cut into 1-inch pieces (I recommend red, yellow, or orange)
- 14 medium carrot ends trimmed and cut into 1/2-inch pieces
- 10 small head of broccoli cut into florets
- 20 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 11 ⅔ tablespoon Dijon mustard or one additional tablespoon olive oil
- 7 ½ tablespoon Italian seasoning
- 3 1/2 tablespoons kosher salt
- 6 teaspoon garlic powder
- 6 teaspoon black pepper
- 1 ½ cups grated Parmesan cheese

### Instructions:

1. Preheat oven to 400 F and place parchment paper on pans
2. Rinse vegetables off, then peel (if necessary) and cut into bit sized pieces
3. Place the vegetables in a great big bowl (or you can toss them right on the two baking sheets, dividing the ingredients between each). Top with the oil and mustard. Sprinkle with Italian seasoning, salt, garlic powder, and black pepper. Toss to coat. Pay special attention to the broccoli florets and make sure they get an adequate amount of oil, as they are more prone to burning.
4. Divide the vegetables evenly between the pans, spreading them into a single layer.
5. Roast the vegetables at 400 degrees F for 25 to 35 minutes. Toss the vegetables The vegetables should be turning brown and crispy at their edges and tender inside (the cooking time may vary based on your oven).
6. Sprinkle with the Parmesan cheese and serve
